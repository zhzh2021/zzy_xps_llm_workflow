LEFT PANEL INTERPRETATION: Chemical Type Effects
================================================================================

WHAT IT SHOWS:
Does your choice of anion (FeCl2 vs FeSO4) or ligand (Malic vs Tartaric acid)
significantly affect Fe coordination structure?

YOUR RESULTS:

1. LIGAND TYPE (Malic Acid → Tartaric Acid)
   
   Effect on "Coordination Order/Disorder" (edge_slope)
   ✓ GREEN BAR: r = +0.41 (INCREASES)
   
   MEANING:
   - Tartaric acid creates MORE ORDERED Fe coordination than malic acid
   - Edge slope measures structural order: steep = crystalline, gentle = amorphous
   - The edge becomes steeper with tartaric acid
   
   WHY THIS HAPPENS:
   - Tartaric acid: HOOC-CH(OH)-CH(OH)-COOH (symmetric, 2 COOH groups)
   - Malic acid:    HOOC-CH2-CH(OH)-COOH (asymmetric, 1 COOH + 1 OH)
   - Tartaric's symmetry creates more regular chelate rings around Fe
   
   PRACTICAL IMPLICATION:
   ► Use tartaric acid for more homogeneous, ordered electrolytes
   ► Use malic acid if you want more structural flexibility/disorder


   Effect on "Average Electronic State" (xanes_centroid)
   ✗ RED BAR: r = -0.39 (DECREASES)
   
   MEANING:
   - Tartaric acid shifts the average XANES energy LOWER
   - XANES centroid = weighted average energy of all transitions
   - Lower centroid = shift toward lower-energy electronic states
   
   PRACTICAL IMPLICATION:
   ► Tartaric acid may stabilize lower oxidation states or create 
     stronger ligand fields that lower transition energies


2. ANION TYPE (FeCl2 vs FeSO4)
   
   NO BARS DISPLAYED
   
   MEANING:
   - No statistically significant correlations detected (p > 0.05)
   - Anion type does NOT directly control Fe inner-sphere structure
   
   WHY THIS HAPPENS:
   - Organic ligands (malic/tartaric acid) dominate the inner coordination sphere
   - Cl⁻ and SO4²⁻ are likely OUTER-SPHERE ions (don't bind directly to Fe)
   - They affect ionic strength but not Fe-ligand bonding
   
   NOTE: ANOVA analysis shows anion type DOES affect overall spectral 
   fingerprints (via edge_slope feature), but not through simple linear 
   correlations with individual structural parameters.

RIGHT PANEL INTERPRETATION: Solution Parameter Effects
================================================================================

WHAT IT SHOWS:
How do continuous experimental variables (pH, concentrations, ratio) 
quantitatively control Fe structure?

YOUR TOP 5 RESULTS (ranked by correlation strength):

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Ligand Concentration → Site Heterogeneity
   GREEN BAR: r = +0.71 (STRONGEST CORRELATION)
   
   MEANING:
   - Higher ligand concentration = MORE HETEROGENEOUS Fe sites
   - Site heterogeneity measured by white_line_fwhm (peak width)
   - Wider peak = greater variety of local Fe environments
   
   MECHANISM:
   - At low [ligand]: Mostly Fe-H2O coordination
   - At high [ligand]: Mix of [Fe(H2O)6]²⁺, [Fe(ligand)1]²⁺, [Fe(ligand)2]²⁺, etc.
   - Multiple coordination complexes coexist → heterogeneous sites
   
   PRACTICAL IMPLICATION:
   ► Low ligand concentration = more uniform Fe environment
   ► High ligand concentration = broader distribution of Fe species
   ► Important for electrochemistry: multiple species may have different 
     redox potentials and kinetics!

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Anion Concentration → Ligand Field Strength
   GREEN BAR: r = +0.59
   
   MEANING:
   - Higher anion concentration = STRONGER ligand field around Fe
   - Ligand field strength measured by white_line_energy
   - Stronger field = greater orbital mixing, higher transition energies
   
   MECHANISM:
   - Higher ionic strength affects Fe-ligand bond lengths
   - Electrostatic screening modulates ligand field strength
   - May also involve Fe-anion secondary coordination
   
   PRACTICAL IMPLICATION:
   ► Anion concentration tunes the electronic structure of Fe-ligand bonds
   ► Can use this to optimize redox potentials

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Anion Concentration → Site Heterogeneity  
   GREEN BAR: r = +0.57
   
   MEANING:
   - Higher anion concentration = MORE HETEROGENEOUS Fe sites
   - Similar mechanism to ligand concentration effect
   
   MECHANISM:
   - High [anion] creates competing equilibria with different anion numbers
   - Mixture of species with varying anion association
   
   PRACTICAL IMPLICATION:
   ► Combined with ligand concentration effect, total ionic strength 
     is a major determinant of site distribution

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Ligand Concentration → Ligand Field Strength
   GREEN BAR: r = +0.49
   
   MEANING:
   - Higher ligand concentration = STRONGER ligand field
   - Direct effect of ligand binding on Fe electronic structure
   
   MECHANISM:
   - More ligands displace water molecules
   - Carboxylates are stronger field ligands than H2O
   - Shifts d-orbital splitting (Δ increases)
   
   PRACTICAL IMPLICATION:
   ► Ligand concentration controls both the NUMBER of ligands coordinated 
     (site heterogeneity, r=0.71) AND the STRENGTH of ligand field (r=0.49)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Ligand Concentration → Coordination Geometry
   RED BAR: r = -0.38
   
   MEANING:
   - Higher ligand concentration CHANGES coordination geometry
   - Negative correlation suggests shift AWAY from baseline geometry
   - Coordination geometry measured by pre_edge_area
   
   MECHANISM:
   - Low [ligand]: Octahedral Fe(H2O)6²⁺ (small pre-edge)
   - High [ligand]: Distorted octahedral or 5-coordinate species
   - Bulky organic ligands cause steric distortions
   
   PRACTICAL IMPLICATION:
   ► Geometry changes affect reactivity and stability
   ► Distorted geometries may be more reactive

KEY SCIENTIFIC INSIGHTS
================================================================================

1. LIGAND CONCENTRATION IS THE PRIMARY CONTROL VARIABLE
   - Strongest correlations (|r| up to 0.71)
   - Controls site heterogeneity, ligand field, and coordination geometry
   - Most important experimental knob for tuning Fe structure

2. LIGAND TYPE (MALIC VS TARTARIC) AFFECTS STRUCTURAL ORDER
   - Tartaric acid → more ordered, homogeneous coordination
   - Malic acid → more disordered, flexible structures
   - r = 0.41 (moderate effect)

3. ANION TYPE (Cl⁻ vs SO4²⁻) HAS MINIMAL DIRECT EFFECT
   - No significant correlations with individual structural parameters
   - Likely outer-sphere ions (don't coordinate directly to Fe)
   - Still affects overall spectral fingerprint through complex mechanisms

4. ANION CONCENTRATION HAS SECONDARY EFFECTS
   - Modulates ligand field strength (r = 0.59)
   - Increases site heterogeneity (r = 0.57)
   - Works through ionic strength and electrostatic effects

5. SITE HETEROGENEITY IS STRONGLY CONCENTRATION-DEPENDENT
   - Both ligand (r = 0.71) and anion (r = 0.57) concentrations matter
   - Multiple Fe coordination complexes coexist at high concentrations
   - Critical for understanding electrochemical behavior

- Site Heterogeneity      → white_line_fwhm (peak width)
- Ligand Field Strength   → white_line_energy (peak position)
- Coordination Geometry   → pre_edge_area (symmetry breaking)
- Coordination Order      → edge_slope (structural order)
- Average Electronic State → xanes_centroid (energy centroid)

These XANES features are quantitative fingerprints that translate directly 
to physical/chemical properties of the Fe coordination environment.
